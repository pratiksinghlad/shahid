Shahid Nadeem Ansari Ehsanoor Rahim was born into a lower middle-class family in Malegaon, a textile town located approximately 300 km from Mumbai. He received his primary education at an Urdu Medium Municipal School and pursued his graduation through a night college while simultaneously working part-time in a power-loom factory to support his family of seven. As the eldest member of the family, he carried significant responsibilities throughout his academic journey.

Shahid completed his law graduation from K.B.H. Law College, Malegaon, on a Government Scholarship and was later enrolled with the Bar Council of Maharashtra & Goa, after completion of Law Graduation he migrated to Mumbai in search of better professional opportunities.

Despite being a law graduate, his initial years in Mumbai were marked by severe struggle. During this period, he worked as an assistant and coordinator in a office while awaiting an opportunity in legal profession. Over the past twelve years, he has been fully devoted to legal practice and regularly appears before the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India and various High Courts across the country.

He has been associated with several landmark cases in Indian judicial history, including the Babri Masjid–Ram Janmabhoomi land dispute, the Triple Talaq case, challenges to the Places of Worship Act, 1991, anti-conversion laws matter, bulldozer action cases and representation of victims in the Malegaon 2008 Bomb Blast case. He frequently appears in matters arising under UAPA, TADA, POTA, NIA laws and Human Rights Violation Cases.

In 2016, Shahid was selected for the International Visitor Leadership Program (IVLP – Judicial) and visited the United States of America to study the U.S. judicial system. During this program, he visited the U.S. Supreme Court, several State Appellate and Trial Courts, and interacted with Justices, Judges, and senior judicial officers.

In 2025, he was appointed as a **Notary Public** by the Ministry of Law & Justice, Government of India.

Presently serving as **Legal Adviser to Jamiat Ulama-I-Hind**, A prominent 100-Year-Old Socio Religious Organisation, he travels extensively across the country providing legal aid to the marginalized and voiceless. His life stands as a testament that background does not determine destiny, hardship does not defeat purpose, and justice, when pursued with sincerity has the power to transform lives.

Beyond the courtroom, he has a keen interest in sports. He earlier played cricket and currently plays lawn tennis for fitness, peace, and recreation.

Shahid occasionally writes article on Legal Affairs in Leading Urdu News Papers and Online Portals, Knowing languages including English, Urdu, Hindi, Marathi, Arabic and Persian etc.

From a power-loom factory to the highest courts of the nation, his journey reminds us:

**Dreams don’t need privilege—only perseverance.**
